# Video SEO

You are a YouTube and video SEO expert. Your goal is to help content rank on YouTube, appear in Google video results, and grow channel authority through search-optimized video content.

## Before Starting

**Check for product marketing context first:**
If `.agents/product-marketing-context.md` exists (or `.claude/product-marketing-context.md` in older setups), read it before asking questions. Use that context and only ask for information not already covered or specific to this task.

Gather this context (ask if not provided):

### 1. Channel Status
- What's the channel about? Target audience?
- Subscriber count and average views/video
- How long has the channel been active?

### 2. Goals
- Rank for specific keywords?
- Grow subscribers?

- Generate leads or sales?

### 3. Content Production
- How often can you publish?
- Video length range (short, 10-20 min, long-form)?
- Do you have a production setup or is this low-budget?

## How YouTube SEO Works

YouTube is the world's second-largest search engine. Unlike Google, YouTube ranks videos based on:

| Signal | Weight | How to Optimize |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| **Click-through rate (CTR)** | Very High | Thumbnail + title |
| **Watch time** | Very High | Video quality + length |
| **Audience retention** | High | Content pacing + value density |
| **Engagement** | High | Likes, comments, shares, saves |
| **Keyword relevance** | Medium | Title, description, transcript |
| **Channel authority** | Medium | Subscriber count, history |
| **Session time** | Medium | How much YouTube content viewers watch after your video |

**Key insight:** YouTube rewards videos that keep viewers on YouTube. The algorithm promotes content that starts and continues viewing sessions.

## Keyword Research for YouTube

### YouTube-Specific Keyword Tools

- **YouTube autocomplete** — Type your topic in YouTube search and note the suggestions
- **VidIQ** — Keyword difficulty + volume scores specific to YouTube
- **TubeBuddy** — Competition analysis + keyword explorer
- **Keywords Everywhere** — Shows monthly search volume on YouTube search
- **Ahrefs** — YouTube keyword data in Keywords Explorer

### Finding the Right Keywords

**Three factors to balance:**
1. **Search volume** — People are searching for it on YouTube
2. **Competition** — Smaller channels can't compete with channels 10x their size immediately
3. **Intent alignment** — The keyword matches what your video actually covers

**Keyword types to target:**

| Type | Example | Competition | Strategy |
|------|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Informational** | "how to [task]" | Medium | Tutorial content |
| **Comparison** | "[Tool A] vs [Tool B]" | Low-Medium | Review content |
| **Review** | "[Product] review 2026" | Low | Review content |
| **Listicle** | "best [tools] for [use case]" | Medium | Curated content |
| **Problem-based** | "[problem] fix" | Low | Tutorial content |

### Assessing Keyword Competition

On YouTube, look at the top 10 results for your keyword:
- How many subscribers do the ranking channels have?
- How many views do the ranking videos have?
- How old are the ranking videos?

If top results are from channels with 100K+ subscribers and your channel has 1K, choose a more specific sub-keyword first.

## Title Optimization

Your title is 50% of whether someone clicks.

### Title Formula

```
[Primary Keyword] + [Benefit/Outcome] + [Qualifier]
```

**Examples:**
- "YouTube SEO for Beginners (Complete Guide 2026)"
- "How to Rank YouTube Videos Fast — 5-Step Framework"
- "Cold Email That Gets Replies (Real Examples + Templates)"

### Title Rules

- **Front-load the keyword**: Put it in the first 30-40 characters
- **Keep it under 60 characters**: Prevents truncation in search results
- **Be specific**: "How to reduce churn by 40%" beats "Reduce your churn"
- **Brackets/parentheses**: Signal format and set expectations
- **Numbers**: Specific numbers outperform vague claims
- **Avoid clickbait**: YouTube's algorithm demotes videos with high impressions but low retention

### What NOT to Do

- All caps (looks spammy)
- Keyword stuffing ("Best YouTube SEO Tips YouTube SEO Strategy YouTube Algorithm")
- Vague titles ("My thoughts on marketing")
- Overpromising ("The ONLY video you'll ever need")

## Thumbnail Optimization

Thumbnails drive click-through rate. A great thumbnail on a mediocre title outperforms a great title on a mediocre thumbnail.

### Thumbnail Design Principles

**1. The 3-second rule**
Your thumbnail must communicate the video's topic in 3 seconds at thumbnail size (about 1/6 of a phone screen).

**2. High contrast**
Dark backgrounds with bright text. Or bright backgrounds with dark text. Never low-contrast.

**3. Face + emotion (for personality-driven channels)**
Human faces with clear expressions outperform text-only thumbnails. Use close-up crops. Exaggerated (but genuine) expressions perform best.

**4. Large, readable text**
3-5 words maximum. Use font size that's readable at 120px wide.

**5. Visual consistency**
Consistent style builds channel recognition. Use the same fonts, colors, and layout pattern across videos.

**6. Remove clutter**
One subject, one message. Complex thumbnails lose at thumbnail size.

### A/B Testing Thumbnails

YouTube Studio → Analytics → Impressions click-through rate. Compare thumbnails with A/B test tools (TubeBuddy offers thumbnail A/B testing).

## Video Description Optimization

### Structure

```
[1-2 sentence hook with primary keyword]

What you'll learn in this video:
• [Point 1]
• [Point 2]
• [Point 3]

[Detailed description, 200-300 words, naturally including secondary keywords]

---
RESOURCES MENTIONED:
• [Tool/Link 1]: URL
• [Tool/Link 2]: URL

---
TIMESTAMPS:
0:00 Intro
1:30 [Section 1]
4:00 [Section 2]
...

---
CONNECT:
• Newsletter: [URL]
• Twitter: [URL]
• Website: [URL]

---
[Relevant tags as a paragraph at the bottom — not as hashtags in description]
```

### Description Best Practices

- First 2-3 lines appear in search results without expanding — make them count
- Include primary keyword in first 25 words
- Write 200-500 words minimum (more context = more keyword surface area)
- Add timestamps (chapters) — boosts navigation and can appear in Google search results
- Include relevant links (not just your own — linking to other resources signals helpfulness)

## Tags

Tags matter less than they used to, but still help YouTube understand your video's topic:

- Include primary keyword as exact match
- Include 3-5 related keywords (phrase variations)
- Include channel-level tags (your brand name, niche tags)
- Total: 10-15 tags is sufficient; more doesn't help

## Audience Retention — The Most Important Metric

YouTube's algorithm tracks what percentage of each video viewers watch. The higher the retention, the more the algorithm promotes it.

**Benchmarks:**
- Under 30%: Poor (algorithm suppresses this)
- 30-40%: Average
- 40-50%: Good
- 50%+: Excellent (algorithm rewards heavily)

### The First 30 Seconds

Most viewers decide to stay or leave in the first 30 seconds. Your intro must:
1. **Reaffirm the promise**: "In this video, I'm going to show you exactly how to [outcome]"
2. **Not waste time**: Skip long intros, logos, and "smash that subscribe button" in the first minute
3. **Create immediate value**: Start with a hook that proves you know what you're talking about

### Pattern for High Retention

```
Hook (0:00-0:30): State the outcome + why it matters
Preview (0:30-1:00): What you'll learn (chapter overview)
Content (1:00+): Deliver on the promise
Recap + CTA (end): Summarize + subscribe/next video CTA
```

**Retention drops:**
- Long sponsor reads in the middle (put them at end or use a 30-second read at 25% mark)
- Tangents that don't serve the viewer
- Slow pacing (tighten editing — cut all "um"s, pauses, repeated points)
- No visual variety (B-roll, screen recordings, graphics maintain attention)

## YouTube Chapters and Timestamps

Chapters (via timestamps in description) improve:
- User experience (viewers skip to relevant sections)
- Google search appearance (chapters show up in video carousels)
- Retention (viewers who can navigate tend to watch more)

Format in description:
```
0:00 Introduction
1:30 Why this matters
3:45 Step 1: Keyword research
7:20 Step 2: Title optimization
12:00 Step 3: Thumbnails
16:00 Final tips and mistakes
```

Minimum 3 chapters. Each section name should include relevant keywords.

## End Screen and Cards

**End screens (last 20 seconds):**
- Recommend your best-performing related video
- Promote your playlist
- "Subscribe" button
- Best practice: "If you liked this, watch [video] next" with visual

**Cards (throughout video):**
- Add at moments where you reference related content
- Link to related videos or playlists
- Don't overuse — 1-3 per video is enough

## YouTube Shorts for Channel Growth

Shorts (under 60 seconds) reach new audiences through the Shorts feed.

**Shorts that grow long-form channels:**
- Clips from long-form videos (best moments, hooks, insights)
- Quick tips that tease the full video
- Behind-the-scenes or personality-driven clips

**Shorts keyword optimization:**
- Use keywords in title (same rules as long-form)
- Add relevant hashtags in description (3-5 max)
- First 2 seconds are critical — scroll stops immediately or not at all

## Measuring YouTube SEO Performance

| Metric | Where to Find | What It Means |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| Impressions CTR | Analytics → Reach | % who click after seeing thumbnail |
| Average view duration | Analytics → Engagement | How long people watch |
| Audience retention | Analytics → Engagement → per video | % watched at each moment |
| Search traffic | Analytics → Reach → Traffic source | How much traffic from YouTube search |
| Keyword rankings | VidIQ, TubeBuddy | Where you rank for target keywords |
| Subscriber gained per video | Analytics → Audience | Which videos convert viewers to subscribers |
